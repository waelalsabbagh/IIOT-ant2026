Workshop Overview

As Industry 4.0 reshapes the landscape of modern manufacturing and industrial operations, the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) has emerged as a pivotal force-interconnecting machines, sensors, and control systems to drive unprecedented levels of efficiency, automation, and data-driven decision-making. While this interconnectedness offers immense operational benefits, it also amplifies cybersecurity risks, especially in safety-critical environments, where even a single security breach can lead to severe operational, financial, and human consequences.

The Workshop on Securing the Industrial Internet of Things brings together leading researchers, industry practitioners, and cybersecurity experts to address these pressing challenges. Participants will explore the latest threat landscapes, share innovative defense strategies, and discuss resilient architectural designs that ensure industrial networks remain robust, secure, and reliable. This forum provides a unique opportunity to advance knowledge, foster collaboration, and shape the future of IIoT security in an increasingly connected industrial world.

Topics of Interest

  • Threat landscape analysis and risk assessment in IIoT environments
  • Secure design and deployment of IIoT devices and systems
  • Intrusion detection and prevention techniques for IIoT networks
  • Cryptographic protocols and techniques for securing IIoT communications
  • Authentication and access control mechanisms for IIoT devices and users
  • Secure firmware and software update mechanisms for IIoT devices
  • Privacy-preserving techniques for IIoT data collection and processing
  • Compliance with industry standards (e.g., NIST, IEC, ISO)
  • Resilience and recovery strategies for IIoT systems
  • Integration of AI and ML for IIoT security enhancement
  • Case studies and real-world implementations

Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished manuscripts in accordance with the guidelines provided on the ANT 2026 website. All submitted papers will undergo peer review by the workshop's Technical Program Committee (TPC). Accepted papers will be published in the ANT 2026 Proceedings (Elsevier Procedia Computer Science) and indexed by Scopus, EI, and others.
