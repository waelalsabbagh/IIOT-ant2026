Workshop Overview

As Industry 4.0 reshapes the landscape of modern manufacturing and industrial operations, the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) has emerged as a pivotal force—interconnecting machines, sensors, control systems and cloud services to enable smarter, more efficient industrial processes.

The Workshop on Securing the Industrial Internet of Things brings together leading researchers, industry practitioners, and cybersecurity experts to address these pressing challenges. Participants will discuss design, deployment, and mitigation strategies for securing IIoT systems.

Topics of Interest

  • Threat landscape analysis and risk assessment in IIoT environments
  • Secure design and deployment of IIoT devices and systems
  • Intrusion detection and prevention techniques for IIoT networks
  • Cryptographic protocols and techniques for securing IIoT communications
  • Authentication and access control mechanisms for IIoT devices and users
  • Secure firmware and software update mechanisms for IIoT devices
  • Privacy-preserving techniques for IIoT data collection and processing
  • Compliance with industry standards (e.g., NIST, IEC, ISO)
  • Resilience and recovery strategies for IIoT systems
  • Integration of AI and ML for IIoT security enhancement
  • Case studies and real-world implementations

Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished manuscripts in accordance with the guidelines provided on the ANT 2026 website. All submitted papers will undergo peer review by the workshop's Technical Program Committee (TPC). Accepted papers will be published in the ANT 2026 Proceedings (Elsevier Procedia Computer Science) and indexed by Scopus, EI, and others.

Workshop Chairs

Dr. Wael Alsabbagh

University of Doha for Science and Technology, Qatar

wael.alsabbagh@udst.edu.qa

Wael Alsabbagh received the M.Sc. degree in Automatic Control and Computer Engineering from Al-Baath University, Homs, Syria, in 2015, and the Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from the Brandenburg University of Technology Cottbus-Senftenberg, Germany, in 2023. From 2018 to 2025, he was a Scientist with the IHP - Leibniz Institut für Innovative Mikroelektronik, Frankfurt (Oder), Germany. Since 2025, he has been an Assistant Professor with the University of Doha for Science and Technology, Qatar. His research interests include cyber-attacks and security, with a focus on mitigation methods for attacks targeting industrial control systems (ICS) and SCADA systems. His research has been recognized with several awards, including the IEEE Outstanding Paper Award for the IEEE Open Journal of the Industrial Electronics Society in 2024. Dr. Alsabbagh also serves as a reviewer for several international conferences and journals, including IEEE Access, IEEE Internet of Things Journal, Computers & Security, and others.
